Retrieval-augmented language agents typically search for evidence that supports
an emerging answer, while reflection remains weakly grounded in retrievable
counter-evidence. We introduce Falsification-Augmented Retrieval (FAR), a
control framework that decomposes an initial answer into dependent claims,
assigns typed evidence requirements, retrieves support, refutation, and boundary
evidence, and revises each claim according to temporal, entity, numerical,
causal, source-reliability, definition, or counter-evidence conflicts. FAR
exposes the claim-evidence map and a before/after revision trace. We also define
FalsiRAG-Bench, a controlled evaluation protocol in which falsifying evidence is
present in the corpus and revision actions are annotated. The current artifact
contains a balanced 300-item machine-seeded candidate set, strict promotion to
double-annotated and adjudicated gold, five transparent baselines, four
component ablations, and paired statistical inference. This draft states the
method and evaluation contract; final performance claims are intentionally
withheld until independent annotation and frozen multi-model experiments are
complete.
